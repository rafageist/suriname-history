# Topic 1: Workers Stand Up for a Better Life

## Practice Exercises: Topic 1 Review

**1.** The following steps about the economic world crisis of the 1930s are in the wrong order. In groups, discuss what the correct order is and write it down. Then one of the groups presents their order to the class. The other groups compare whether they have the same order.

1. There is an economic crisis.
2. There is much employment in businesses and factories.
3. Poverty and unemployment rise.
4. The production of businesses and factories increases.
5. Businesses and factories can no longer sell their products.
6. In America and Europe, there is a period of prosperity at the beginning of the 20th century.
7. Workers are laid off, and businesses and factories go bankrupt.

**2.** In a business or a factory, there are employees and employers. In class, discuss with each other whether employees and the employer want the same thing.

- a. What does the employer want?
- b. What do the employees want?

**3.** Suppose: you would establish a trade union in class for the interests of the students. For example, you discuss with each other how many tests and homework you want per week.

- a. What do the students want?
- b. Do you think the teacher will agree or disagree with your proposal?
- c. What does the teacher want?
- d. What does the teacher have to take into account?
- e. Can you come to an agreement?

**4.** In image 7, it can be seen that the street where Anton de Kom used to live was named after him. The university in our country was also named after him.

- a. Why do you think streets, buildings, and schools are named after people?
- b. Is your school also named after someone? If yes, who?
