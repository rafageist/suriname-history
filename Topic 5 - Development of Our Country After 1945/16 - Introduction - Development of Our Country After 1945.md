INTRODUCTION

In this theme, you will learn more about some plans for the development of our country after 1945. The first lesson explains why development aid came. It also writes about some projects during the Prosperity Fund. The second lesson is about the construction of the dam at Brokopondo and the consequences for the people and animals that lived in that area. The last lesson covers social development, which was also part of the development plans.

### Teacher's Guide - Answers and Explanations

Below you can find the quarterly assignments for the first quarter.

From the following four assignments, you choose at least one assignment that you will carry out together with the students.

1. COLLAGE

Goal: Creatively depict a subject and gain insight into it.

Group size: Two to four students.

Duration: Three class periods.

The assignment is given and explained. Students get time to work on it at home and also in class.

Materials: Photos, texts, newspaper clippings, old magazines, markers, colored pencils, scissors, glue sticks, and paper. You and/or students bring these materials from home.

Evaluation rubric.

Method: During the first class period, the assignment is explained and groups are formed.

Each group chooses a topic from the themes already covered. For example, a collage can be made about theme 2 (education in our country) or theme 3 (different cultures in our country).

You discuss the topic with the students. Ask some questions about the theme that has been covered and agree on what requirements the collage must meet.

Students look for photos and images, for example, in (old) newspapers and magazines. They can also search for information in their textbooks and write something themselves or make drawings.

From the collected material, collages are creatively made, which are displayed in the classroom.

Evaluation: The method and results are discussed as a class.

Assessment: Use the evaluation rubric to assess this assignment. Observe 'Assignment execution,' 'Assignment content,' and 'Listening attitude.'

---

2. CARD GAMES

Goal: The goal of this assignment is to recognize, take apart, and put back together information around a (given) topic.

Group size: The class is divided into groups of four.

Duration: Three to four class periods and time to work at home.

Materials: Thin cardboard to cut out cards, scissors, drawing supplies, optionally (stickers) pictures and glue or tape.

Evaluation rubric.

Method: The number of groups and topics depends on the number of students in the class.

Each group of four receives a subtopic or question from the covered material. To refresh memory, you can briefly summarize the chosen theme. If you choose, for example, theme 3 (different cultures in our country), each group of four can choose a different culture and make four cards about it. Examples of what can be depicted on the cards are:

- Traditional costumes
- Places of worship
- Dishes
- Dance
- Musical instruments

Each group of four makes four cards. On each card, there is a photo or drawing that depicts their topic. This is first discussed within the group, but then everyone can work individually on their card. Pictures from magazines or the internet can be used. Students can also make their own drawing and decorate the cards. However, it should be noted that the four cards of a group form a collection of the given topic to form a set. To recognize the set, the edges of the four cards can be marked with the same color, and the topic is clearly stated at the top of the cards.

Evaluation: Each group of four presents its set, with each student individually telling about their card.

After the presentation of the entire set, it can be played several times in the classroom in small groups of three to five students.

Assessment: Use the evaluation rubric to assess this assignment. Observe 'Assignment execution,' 'Assignment content,' 'Presentation attitude,' and 'Listening attitude.'

---

3. ROLE-PLAY

Goal: The goal of this assignment is to develop empathy for how things were in the past. Social skills such as listening to each other and cooperating are also practiced.

Group size: Depending on the number of students and the chosen topic, as a class or in multiple groups.

Duration: Approximately four class periods (depending on the number of groups).

The role-play itself can last 10 to 15 minutes.

Materials: Depending on the chosen topic, you or the students can bring or make material from home.

Evaluation rubric.

Method: You explain the assignment. Depending on the chosen topic and the number of participants, roles are distributed. If there are multiple groups in the class, each group can be chosen to play a different topic. To refresh memory, you can briefly summarize the chosen themes.

The material offers multiple possibilities for role-play. For example:

- In theme 1, for example, the demonstration of workers can be depicted and the arrest of Louis Doedel.
- In theme 1, the Advice Bureau, which Anton de Kom founded, can also be chosen. One can also depict how he was arrested and locked up and ultimately deported from the country on a boat.
- In theme 2, it can be depicted how education was previously given to Indigenous or enslaved children.
- Theme 3 offers different possibilities for a short play about culture.

In this assignment, you have the task to help with distributing the roles. Optionally, students can distribute the roles themselves within their group. But your role as facilitator/director is important. Also to explain what can and may roughly be played. Together with the group, you discuss how the topic will be depicted.

There is brief practice, during which you give some additional advice. Students get time to study their role.

Evaluation: The short play is performed. You watch together with the other students. After each short play, it is discussed in a circle conversation. Here, other students may say what they thought was good and nice, whether they understood what was depicted, and what could perhaps have been done differently.

Assessment: Use the evaluation rubric to assess this assignment. Observe 'Assignment execution,' 'Assignment content,' and 'Listening attitude.'
