### Student Textbook Content

PROCESSING ASSIGNMENTS

1. In class, through role-play, the independence celebration of our country can be reenacted. Together with the teacher, the roles are distributed and then played. Various points around the independence of our country on November 25 can be included. For example, one can think of:
   - presenting the new Surinamese flag.
   - Johan Ferrier being appointed as the first president of our country.
   - singing our national anthem.
   - telling about the new Constitution that our country received and what is stated in it, among other things.

2. In pairs, describe in your own words what is meant by the following concepts from this theme:
   - Constitution
   - Armed Forces
   - Coup d'État
   - Military Authority
   - Revolution
   - Referendum

3. Make in your notebook a timeline of the period in this theme and write down the years and events that are mentioned in this theme.

4. The following board game can be played by two to five students. For the game, a dice is needed and a pawn for each player. Place your pawn on one of the colored squares (see game board below). The purpose of the game is to get your pawn exactly on the yellow star at the top. Along the way, there are squares with a question mark. You cannot just pass over these. You must roll exactly the right number with the dice to land on the square with the question mark. Then you must answer a question. If your answer is correct, you may continue on the next turn. Otherwise, you skip a turn.

   The first player who reaches the star wins.

   Questions:
   1. When did our country become independent?
   2. Who was the first president of our country?
   3. Name one civil right.
   4. Name one civil duty.
   5. Name the three national symbols of our country.
   6. When did the coup d'État take place in our country?
   7. Where does the Revolution Monument stand?
   8. What does the Revolution Monument commemorate?
   9. What is meant by a revolution?
   10. When did the Civil War begin in our country?
   11. What does the Moiwana Monument commemorate?
   12. With which agreement did the Civil War end?
   13. What is meant by a referendum?
   14. In which year did our country receive a new Constitution?
   15. How many members does The National Assembly consist of?

   You can also think of other questions yourselves that are related to the theme.
