# Topic 1: Workers Stand Up for a Better Life

## Lesson 3: Trade Unions and Trade Union Federations

In the previous lesson, you read that the government acted harshly against people who stood up for workers. There were not yet laws that gave workers the right to organize. Therefore, the government could ban workers' organizations. Public meetings or lectures were also not allowed. Leaders were arrested and punished. For example, Louis Doedel was locked up in a mental asylum for the rest of his life, and Anton de Kom was banished from the country.

With such measures, it is understandable that some people preferred not to come forward. That does not mean they were not dissatisfied! An example of this are letters that appeared in a newspaper in our country between 1932 and 1935. These letters were signed with the name Bok Sark. Bok Sark was a pseudonym. This person was actually a man. He presented himself as a Javanese woman living on a plantation and wrote about the poor conditions and wage cuts. The government tried to find this woman, but no one knew who she was. Only much later, in 1992, a researcher discovered that these letters were actually written by a man.

#### ASSIGNMENT

- Read the text: from which words does it appear that the Javanese contract workers were angry about wage cuts?
- Explain whether writing such a letter is also a form of protest.
- Why do you think the writer used a pseudonym to sign the letters?

In the years around 1930, it was a difficult time to stand up for the interests of workers and the unemployed. Workers and the unemployed had no rights, and the government acted harshly when they protested. When Louis Doedel, together with others, founded the SAWO (Surinaamse Arbeiders en Werknemers Organisatie - Surinamese Workers' Organization) in 1932, this was soon banned by the government. Yet this was one of the first trade unions. Trade unions are associations of employees who do the same work or work in the same company. Employees are people who are employed by a company or an employer. The employer is often the owner of a company.

The goal of a trade union is to stand up together for better working conditions. Examples of this are:

- Better working hours
- Prohibition of child labor
- Good health insurance
- Wage increase
- A good pension

When employees are dissatisfied with their working conditions, the trade union can speak with the employer. But sometimes the employer refuses to meet the demands of the trade union. The trade union and the employer then no longer listen to each other. In such a case, the trade union can call for a strike. Employees lay down their work and demand that the employer improve the working conditions.

In the 20th century, more and more workers organized themselves into trade unions. In the second half of the 20th century, many small trade unions began to unite into larger organizations. These organizations are trade union federations. In an action or strike by one trade union, that union can receive support from other trade unions affiliated with the same federation. Together, you often achieve more than alone.

Three trade union federations united in RAVAKSUR.

In our country, there are various trade union federations. Because organization and cooperation are so important, these federations have also united into one organization. On May 1, 1987, RAVAKSUR (Raad van Vakcentrales in Suriname - Council of Trade Union Federations in Suriname) was established.

There were more and more trade unions in our country. There were trade unions of workers at private companies, but also trade unions of workers who worked for the government. For the trade union members, it was important that the leadership had enough knowledge of trade union matters. Therefore, courses were set up to learn trade union work. A trade union school even emerged in 1969 where trade union people were trained. That is SIVIS (Scholings Instituut voor de Vakbeweging in Suriname - Training Institute for the Trade Union Movement in Suriname). In 2013, a bust of Louis Doedel was placed in front of the building that houses SIVIS.

#### ASSIGNMENT

- In front of which building was the bust of Louis Doedel placed?
- Why do you think they chose to place the bust of Louis Doedel here?

#### REMEMBER

- The government acted harshly against the first trade union leaders.
- The dissatisfaction of the people was also visible in letters in the newspaper.
- SAWO was one of the first trade unions.
- Trade unions are associations that stand up for better working conditions.
- Trade unions can speak with the employer. If this does not help, the trade union can call a strike by the employees.
- Trade unions are united in trade union federations. The trade union federations in our country are united in RAVAKSUR.
- At the trade union school SIVIS, trade union people can be trained.
